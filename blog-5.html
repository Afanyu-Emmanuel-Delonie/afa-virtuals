Behind the Scenes: A Day in the Life of a Virtual Assistant

By Afanyu Emmanuel
July 1, 2025
5 min read
Virtual Assistant, Workflow

You’ve probably heard about virtual assistants — but what do they actually do all day?

Are they just managing emails and calendars, or is there more to the story?

Today, we’re taking you behind the scenes to show you what a typical day looks like for a professional VA at Afa Virtuals. Spoiler: it’s far more strategic and valuable than most people think.

Table of Contents

  • 8:00 AM – Morning Brief & Priorities
  • 9:30 AM – Inbox Cleanup & Client Comms
  • 11:00 AM – Calendar & Scheduling
  • 12:30 PM – Creative or Specialized Tasks
  • 2:00 PM – Admin & Reporting
  • 3:30 PM – Client Check-In or Team Coordination
  • 5:00 PM – Wrap-Up & Handoff
  • Final Thoughts

8:00 AM – Morning Brief & Priorities

  • Review the client’s calendar and inbox
  • Flag urgent messages
  • Set up reminders or task lists using tools like Notion, Trello, or Asana
  • Check in on any scheduled social posts or pending design requests

Outcome

The client starts their day with zero digital clutter.

9:30 AM – Inbox Cleanup & Client Comms

  • VA filters through 100+ emails:
  • Deletes spam
  • Replies to routine inquiries
  • Drafts replies for client approval
  • Organizes messages by folders or labels

Client Feedback

Many clients say their inbox goes from “chaos” to “zen” within days.

11:00 AM – Calendar & Scheduling

  • Books appointments
  • Sends reminders
  • Coordinates with multiple stakeholders
  • Ensures no double-bookings or timezone mix-ups

Real Result

One client saved 4 hours/week just from calendar cleanup.

12:30 PM – Creative or Specialized Tasks

  • Design a social media graphic
  • Draft captions and hashtags
  • Upload and schedule posts
  • Create branded presentation slides
  • Prepare a lead generation list

Multi-Skilled

Many Afa Virtuals VAs are multi-skilled — from Canva and copywriting to CRM management.

2:00 PM – Admin & Reporting

  • Update CRMs like HubSpot or Notion
  • Log expenses or track KPIs
  • Generate short reports (e.g., Instagram growth, client onboarding status)
  • Upload files, organize folders

Result

The client always knows where things stand — without checking.

3:30 PM – Client Check-In or Team Coordination

  • Short voice notes or Loom videos sent to client
  • Sync with other VAs or specialists for project coordination
  • Use Slack, WhatsApp, or email — whatever platform the client prefers

Adaptability

Afa Virtuals VAs adapt to your ecosystem — not the other way around.

5:00 PM – Wrap-Up & Handoff

  • Review progress
  • Leave a status update or daily summary
  • Set reminders or prep for tomorrow’s tasks

Proactive Planning

By the time you clock out, your next day is already mapped out.

Final Thoughts

A virtual assistant isn’t just “remote help.” They’re your behind-the-scenes productivity partner — one who helps you scale without stress.
